At UNICEF, we take the responsibility of handling Zakat extremely seriously. From donations to payments, our programmes, policies and processes are reviewed and vetted by Islamic Scholars and Islamic Philanthropy experts to ensure that the requirements of Zakat are fully met. All Zakat Funds are collected in a dedicated interest-free bank account within UNICEF and will be restricted only to Zakat eligible programmes and projects. By giving your Zakat to the UNICEF Zakat Fund, you’ll be helping vulnerable families in Yemen and Afghanistan get access to a range of vital goods and services, gain temporary household income to facilitate their transition through multiple shocks and provide resources for displaced populations to return to their home areas.

Zakat Compliance

  • Scholars: the Fund is Zakat compliant and has been certified as such by our Islamic scholar. 
  • Infrastructure: follows recommendations by our Islamic scholar and experts by dedicating funds for the benefit of vulnerable families who are eligible for Zakat.
  • Compliance review: annual field visits to monitor the distribution and issue compliance reports. 
  • Donor centric: the destination of the funds is decided by the Zakat payer/ partner at the onset of the partnership. 

Assessment and Monitoring

  • Vulnerability assessment framework: to identify eligible beneficiaries, updated on yearly basis. 
  • Innovation: use of technology in distribution, to increase efficiency and minimize fraud and duplication. 
  • Post distribution monitoring: twice a year, to measure impact and improve implementation.

Financial Governance

  • Management & accountability: funds are kept in a dedicated interest-free bank account in Geneva. 
  • Traceability: all funds are dedicated to Zakat compliant programmes in identified countries with high needs. 
  • Transparency: UNICEF publishes annual Zakat reports, and partners are provided with customized reports.
