Elections

    Nominations Closed

    • 2021 Bylaws Proposed Changes Election

      As a part of the PSF’s mandate to, “to support and facilitate the growth of a diverse and international community of Python programmers”, in June 2020, the Python Software Foundation Board of Directors began an open discussion with the community regarding Board Election reform. The feedback provided highlighted several topics of interest, including the continued need to invest in diversity and inclusion efforts for the Python open source community.


      One of the ways the Board has been acting on this feedback has included the creation of a Diversity and Inclusion Workgroup in December 2020 https://pyfound.blogspot.com/2020/12/announcing-psf-diversity-and-inclusion.html. Additionally the Board has continued to have ongoing discussions about how the Board itself can make adjustments to meet the community’s feedback.


      The PSF Board is proposing changes to the bylaws to be voted on by the membership at the upcoming elections. Discussions regarding term limits are still ongoing within the Board.


      These changes impact the following sections of the PSF’s bylaws:

      • Implementing Section 5.15 Limits on Co-affiliation of Board Members
      • Adjusting Section 4.8. Fellows

      Voting began June 22, 2021 AoE and ballots must be cast before July 8, 2021 AoE


      Below are details for each bullet point.

      Section 5.15 Limits on Co-affiliation of Board Members

      Resolution approved by the board of directors

      RESOLVED, that the Python Software Foundation board approve the bylaw implementation of 'Section 5.15. Limits on Co-affiliation of Board Members' as proposed here, to be voted on by the membership during the next election.

      Context

      The board is focused on promoting a diverse and international community. Many directors work at organizations that are part of our community, sponsor the PSF, and/or volunteer in some other way. It happens occasionally that several board members are employed by the same organization, and this shouldn’t be an impediment for their service. At the same time the Board wants to guard against a situation where a single entity has complete control over the PSF by gaining a majority of the board seats. It’s not quite enough to declare allegiances and let voting members decide who to elect, because for example one company taking over another could result in a majority on the Board. The Bylaw changes aim to prevent such Board capture.

      Section 4.8. Fellows

      Resolution approved by the board of directors

      RESOLVED, that the Python Software Foundation board approve the bylaw updates to 'Section 4.8. Fellows' as proposed here, to be voted on by the membership during the next election.

      Context

      • The PSF Board noticed that the current bylaws lacked the ability for the board to fully delegate Fellow approvals to the Fellows Work Group. Going forward, the board prefers an option to empower the WG (which is composed of only Fellow members) to accept nominations, vote on them, and officially approve them.
      • If these changes are approved, the pull request will be made against the official bylaw repository.
