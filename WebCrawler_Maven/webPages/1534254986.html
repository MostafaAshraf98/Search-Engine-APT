Monthly Outlook

 Last updated 15:43 BST on Friday 13 May

Summary

Unsettled next week but mainly calmer thereafter

Quite an unsettled pattern will set in later this weekend with some wet and possibly very windy days particularly in western areas of the country. We may get the odd thunderstorm as well. Conditions should become more mixed by next weekend. In late May we expect the southern half of the country to have a more settled pattern with slightly above average temperatures. Scotland could still see a few wet and windy days. In June the pattern is on the drier side in England and Wales, more seasonal elsewhere. Temperatures look a little above for early summer.

Saturday 14 May to Sunday 22 May

Unsettled with some thunderstorms and windy days

Saturday starts fair across England, Wales and Northern Ireland with light, intermittent rain across western and northern parts of Scotland, dying out in the early afternoon. Showers with a risk of thunderstorms could develop across southern England and Wales in the afternoon and evening. Sunday should remain fair across Scotland and Northern Ireland. Any showers in England and Wales should die out in the afternoon except in southern England which could see more showery rain and thunderstorms in the evening. Temperatures over the weekend should be a little above average for mid-May. Monday should see rain with the odd thunderstorm moving in from the south through the morning and strengthening winds across Northern Ireland and western parts of the country. Scotland should not see any rain until the afternoon, clearing by Tuesday morning. The rest of the country starts dry with breezy conditions. Throughout the morning further rain could develop over western areas, with some heavier outbreaks in the evening. On Wednesday a windy, wet pattern develops through the early morning across Northern Ireland and the northern half of the mainland. Some heavy bursts are possible with the risk of thunderstorms. The rain should clear through the afternoon with largely fair conditions by the evening, though it may remain breezy across Northern Ireland. Thursday should be a settled day across England, Wales and eastern Scotland. Northern Ireland and western Scotland could see some showery rain at times and heightened winds. By midday on Friday some outbreaks of rain could move across south-east England clearing in the afternoon. Meanwhile, further rain could affect Northern Ireland and Scotland by early afternoon and lasting into the evening. Winds should ease across Northern Ireland and western Scotland. Temperatures should remain above average with the south-east feeling quite warm in the middle part of the week. Next weekend could see a settled pattern across the southern half of the country, with patchy rain elsewhere.

Monday 23 May to Sunday 29 May

Possibly an unsettled few days later in the week

This week could start with Azores high pressure extending towards the British Isles while low pressure moves across Iceland and Scandinavia. The high may even drift over the country for a time over the first half of the week. Towards the latter part of the week the high could retreat further south-west allowing for a few days of more unsettled weather. The confidence in the forecast for this is lower than normal with the models suggesting various possibilities for this week.

The early part of the week could start with a settled pattern across Northern Ireland, Wales and England, while Scotland could see a few showers. Some long sunny spells are possible especially over the southern half of the country. Temperatures look to be a little above average overall.

Over the later part of the week and the weekend confidence is getting lower. We favour a return to a wetter, windier pattern but not too extreme. Periods of enhanced rain or showers would be more likely across the northern half of the country. Southern England could potentially remain dry. Winds could strengthen the over the weekend as well, giving a cooler feel overall.

Monday 30 May to Sunday 12 June

The start of meteorological summer

Into early summer we expect high pressure is across the central northern Atlantic that extends into north-west Europe. Low pressure could be centred west of Iberia and further low pressure systems move across northern Scandinavia. This would bring a drier pattern across England and Wales. Rain could follow a more seasonal pattern across Northern Ireland and Scotland. Some windy days are possible across Scotland but it should not be on the extreme side. Slightly above average temperatures are forecast for most regions.

Over the last week of the forecast confidence is low overall. Once again we have tended towards the historical information and current global weather patterns for this week.
We favour Atlantic high pressure extending towards western Europe. Lower pressure moves across northern Scandinavia and north-west Russia. This would bring a near seasonal temperature pattern. Dry in England and Wales but a more average precipitation pattern in Ireland and Scotland.
There are other possibilities over the latter section of this forecast. During the final week the low pressure may be deeper and further south across Scandinavia. The Atlantic high could be favoured further west. This is a cooler, wetter, windier pattern. We estimate this risk at 35%.

Further ahead

With a new set of data, we should get a more confident picture of the weather over late May and early June including the long bank holiday weekend.
