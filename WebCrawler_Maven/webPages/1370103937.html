UNICEF Zakat Fund FAQ

UNICEF works in over 190 countries and territories to save childrens lives, to defend their rights, and to help them fulfil their potential, from early childhood through adolescence. And we never give up. UNICEF has built an unprecedented global support system for the worlds children.

UNICEF relentlessly works day in and day out to deliver the essentials that give every child an equitable chance in life: health care and immunizations, safe water and sanitation, nutrition, education, emergency relief and more.

Zakat is one of the Five Pillars of Islam and is a specific form of alms that is obligatory for any Muslim who is in possession of wealth over a certain threshold. Zakat is applicable at a rate of 2.5% (or 1/40) of a Muslim's total savings and wealth above a minimum amount known as nisab and is calculated and paid annually.

There are eight categories of people / society who are entitled to receive Zakat funds and they are to be found in a single verse of the Quran (9.60) {Alms are meant only for the poor, the needy, those who administer them, those whose hearts need winning over, to free those in bondage and help those in debt, for Gods cause, and for wayfarers. This is ordained by God; God is all knowing and wise. The majority of the people UNICEF supports fall under at least four of these categories: the poor, the needy, those in bonded labour and the wayfarers.

As an organization supported entirely by voluntary contributions, UNICEF relies more than ever on the engagement and support of all stakeholders, including of religious communities.

Our goal is to provide people of Muslim faith with a mechanism that enables them to entrust UNICEF with their Zakat, so that we can implement projects and programmes on their behalf in an authentic, respectful manner.

UNICEF takes the responsibility of handling Zakat very seriously, which is why we have created a Zakat Fund to support Zakat eligible projects in Muslim-majority communities and countries.

With over 75 years of experience supporting children to survive and thrive across the world, UNICEF has developed the partnerships, reach, infrastructure, and technical know-how, powered by committed and dedicated staff, to reach children and communities in need, especially in Muslim-majority countries.

Zakat Funds are collected in a specific, dedicated?Interest?free bank account?within UNICEF and payments are restricted to clearly identified Zakat eligible programmes and projects.?

Zakat Funds are utilised to uphold the Zakat ethos and principles of providing direct support to eligible individuals in the form of tangible?goods, services and practical support, e.g., cash assistance, food and nutrition, and with more indirect / support such as education, training and health care.

Zakat funds used in project interventions could be in full or part payment, depending on the project activities. Components of any project which are not Zakat eligible will be funded from other sources of income, for example, Sadaqa or unrestricted funds.

Our programmes and processes are reviewed and vetted by Islamic Philanthropy experts, together with Islamic Scholars to ensure the requirements of Zakat are adhered to.

The journey of Zakat Funds from the Zakat Payer to Recipient

  1. Zakat eligible projects are identified which provide direct support and assistance to families within the respective communities in countries that we operate in and are designed on the basis of in-country needs assessments
  2. Zakat funds are held in an interest-free bank account
  3. Zakat funds are transferred to the country office and depending on the type of project provide the relevant benefit to the recipient, for example:
    • In a cash assistance program, a benefit in the form of cash or vouchers is given directly to the recipient
    • In a nutrition programme, Zakat funds are used to purchase food, and this is given directly to the recipient

Zakat payments are allocated, spent, and reported upon within one year of receipt.?

The Shariah Scholar and the Islamic philanthropy experts consulted by UNICEF have advised that the administrators of Zakat are one of the eight different permitted categories of Zakat beneficiaries (Al-Amilina Alayha). As an administrator of Zakat, UNICEF can therefore allocate up to one eight (12.5%) towards the administrative and support costs. UNICEF will apply its standard recovery costs which fall within these criteria. (http://www.undocs.org/DP/FPA-ICEF-UNW/2020/1)

For this initial campaign, UNICEF is being guided by the scholarly guidance of Shaykh Haytham Tamim. However, as we continue our journey to become Zakat-compliant and to create more opportunities to reach beneficiaries in need, UNICEF is also committed to engage with a growing network of highly respected scholars from across countries and regions.

Shaykh Haytham Tamim is a highly respected scholar. He is the founder and director of Shariah Solutions Limited, a consultancy firm in Islamic finance. His expertise spans over 30 years across the main Islamic jurisprudence schools of thought and different Islamic denominations. He has served on the boards of Financial Institutions and Zakat Foundations and has developed Shariah compliant family wealth planning solutions. He recently carried out research on Islamic SDGs with the Islamic Finance Council (IFC) and participated in workshops regarding the same with SOAS, Hamad Bin Khalifa (HBK) University and the University of East London (UEL).

At UNICEF, we take the responsibility of handling Zakat extremely seriously. From donations to payments, our programmes, policies and processes are reviewed and vetted by Islamic Scholars and Islamic Philanthropy experts to ensure that the requirements of Zakat are fully met. All Zakat Funds are collected in a dedicated interest-free bank account within UNICEF and will be restricted only to Zakat eligible programmes and projects. UNICEF has also created a dedicated UNICEF Islamic Fund that enables us to respond to emergency situations and support through Zakat eligible development projects.

By giving your Zakat to the UNICEF Zakat Fund, youll be helping vulnerable families in Afghanistan and Yemen get access to a range of vital goods and services, gain temporary household income to facilitate their transition through multiple shocks and provide resources for displaced populations to return to their home areas.
